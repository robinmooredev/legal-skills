# Acme Corp Vendor Contract Review Playbook

**Version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 2026-03-23
**Document type:** MSA

## Introduction

This playbook defines Acme Corp's standard review criteria for vendor contracts. It was generated by analyzing the team's redlined contracts using the Generate Playbook skill. Use it with the Review Contract with Playbook skill to review any new vendor agreement.

---

## Term & Renewal

### Auto Renewal

**key:** `auto_renewal`
**Expected:** should_be_absent

**What to look for:**
Check whether the contract automatically renews at the end of the initial term. Look for language like "shall automatically renew," "successive renewal terms," or "unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal." Auto-renewal clauses are typically in the Term section. Also check the order form — it may override the MSA's renewal terms.

**Order form language:**
"Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in the Agreement, this Order Form and the Agreement shall not automatically renew. Any renewal shall require a new Order Form executed by both parties."

## Data Protection & Privacy

### Use of Customer Data for AI Training

**key:** `vendor_ai_training`
**Expected:** should_be_absent

**What to look for:**
Check whether the vendor reserves the right to use customer data (or derivatives like anonymized/aggregated data) to train machine learning or AI models. This is often buried in the data use or license grant sections, sometimes framed as "service improvement." Also check the DPA and privacy policy for conflicting language.

**Order form language:**
"Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in the Agreement, Vendor shall not use Customer Data, including anonymized or aggregated derivatives thereof, for the purpose of training, improving, or developing machine learning or artificial intelligence models. For the avoidance of doubt, 'service improvement' as used in the Agreement does not include training or development of AI/ML models on Customer Data."

## Data Lifecycle

### Data Deletion Obligations

**key:** `no_data_deletion_guarantee`
**Expected:** should_be_present

**What to look for:**
Confirm the vendor commits to deleting customer data after termination, within a specific timeframe, upon written request. Look for deletion obligations in the data processing, termination, or survival sections. If the contract says data is "retained for legitimate business purposes" or grants a perpetual license to retained data, that's a fail.

**Order form language:**
"Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Vendor shall, within thirty (30) days of Customer's written request, delete all Customer Data in Vendor's possession or control, including all copies and backups, and certify such deletion in writing. Vendor shall not retain Customer Data beyond the deletion period except as required by applicable law."
